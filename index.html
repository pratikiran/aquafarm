What is Aquaponics?

Aquaponics is a unique and sustainable food production method combining aquaculture (raising fish) with hydroponics (growing plants in water). In this system, the waste produced by fish is broken down by beneficial bacteria and converted into nutrients that plants absorb. The plants, in turn, filter the water, which is then returned to the fish. This creates a closed-loop system that uses 90% less water than traditional agriculture and can produce fish and vegetables in a small space.

Mission Statement
